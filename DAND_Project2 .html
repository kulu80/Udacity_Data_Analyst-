Introduction to Data Analysis Project¶

The objective of this project is to investigate the Titanic dataset which contains the information for 891 people of the 2224 passangers on board of the Titanic that sank in North Atlantic Ocean in the early morning of 15 April, 1912, after colliding with an iceberg during her maiden voyage from Southampton to New York City Wikipidia. The objective of this investigation is to answer the following quastions based on the information provided in the dataset.¶

  1. Who are the demography of the passangers onbaord (i.e, interms of sex, age, class etc.?
  2. Which deck were the passagers on and how does that relate to their class?
  3. Where did the passanges come from? (e.g, city, country etc)
  4. What factors helped someone to survive

In conclusion attempts were made to analyse the titanic data which provides information for 891 passangers among the 2224 passanger who were on board during the trip. Which only containts information about 40% of the total passangers on board. Even though it could be possible to drive important insights from the dataset, it is difficult to draw conclusion based on the information as which factor contributed to survival fo passanger on board.So the analysis based on the avialable information from the dataset reveals that three factor:-sex, class and age are more correlated with the survival of the passanger. Due to the know fact that females and youngester are given priority for rescue it appears that sex and age are most important factors to detrmine survival. Class also has an important role for survival as first class passangers are more chance of get priorty for rescue. Given the fact that, most of the 'Cabin' information is missed it is really difficult to draw conclusion based on this information about survival rate. On the dataset, although it contains not all the information about all passangers of the 891 and all passangers on board of Titanic, it is a very organized and simplistic data set for starting to work with data analysis, and that prepares me to deal with a complex and more messy dataset of different format in my future data science career, provided that I would becoma a data scientist.¶
